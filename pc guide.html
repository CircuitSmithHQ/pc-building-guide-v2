A Beginner's Guide to Building Your First Gaming PC

Welcome to the world of PC building! This interactive guide will walk you through assembling your computer from scratch. It might seem daunting, but by following these steps, you'll have a custom-built rig you can be proud of. Use the navigation on the left to jump to any step.

Step 1: Pre-Build Checklist

Before you start, make sure you have everything ready. This prevents you from stopping mid-build to look for a missing part or tool. Check off items as you gather them.

Step 2: Preparing the Motherboard

1. Install the CPU

Locate the socket, lift the lever, align the CPU's gold arrow with the socket's arrow, and gently drop it in. Do not force it. Lower the lever to secure it.

2. Install the RAM

Open the clips on the RAM slots. Align the notch on the RAM stick with the notch in the slot. Push down firmly on both ends until the clips click shut.

3. Install the M.2 SSD

If you have one, locate the M.2 slot. Insert the drive at an angle, press it down, and secure it with the small screw.

Step 3: Installing the Motherboard in the Case

1. Install the I/O Shield

If it's not pre-installed, snap the I/O shield into the rectangular cutout on the back of your case from the inside.

2. Install Standoffs

Screw standoffs into the case holes that align with your motherboard's mounting holes. These prevent short circuits.

3. Mount the Motherboard

Carefully lower the board onto the standoffs, aligning the ports with the I/O shield. Screw it in securely but not overly tight.

Step 4: Installing Other Components

1. Install the Power Supply (PSU)

Slide the PSU into its bay (usually at the bottom rear of the case) and screw it in from the outside.

2. Install the Graphics Card (GPU)

Remove the case brackets corresponding to the top PCIe slot. Align the card with the slot and press down firmly until it clicks. Screw its bracket to the case.

3. Install Storage Drives

Mount any 2.5" or 3.5" drives into the case's drive bays and secure them with screws.

Step 5: Connecting the Cables

Power Cables

Connect the large 24-pin motherboard cable and the 8-pin CPU cable (top-left of motherboard). Connect the PCIe power cables to your GPU if required.

Front Panel Cables

These tiny cables for your case's power button, USB ports, etc., connect to a block of pins on the motherboard. Check your motherboard manual for the exact layout!

Fans & Drives

Connect case fans to the fan headers on the motherboard. Connect SATA data and power cables to your storage drives.

Step 6: First Boot and OS Installation

Connect your monitor cable to the graphics card, not the motherboard. Plug in the PC and press the power button. If it turns on and shows a display, you're ready to insert your OS installation USB and follow the on-screen prompts. Congratulations, you've built a PC!

Basic Troubleshooting

  • Is the power switch on the back of the PSU flipped on?
  • Are all power cables (motherboard, CPU) firmly connected?
  • Are the front panel power switch cables connected correctly to the motherboard?
  • Is the monitor cable plugged into the graphics card, not the motherboard?
  • Is the graphics card fully seated in its slot? Does it have power connected?
  • Try reseating the RAM sticks. Push them out and click them back in firmly.
